GENERALIZED ANXIETY DISORDER 7-ITEM SCALE (GAD-7)
==================================================

The GAD-7 is a self-administered patient questionnaire used as a screening tool and severity measure for Generalized Anxiety Disorder.

GAD-7 Questions (Symptom Frequency Over the Last 2 Weeks):
1. Feeling nervous, anxious, or on edge.
2. Not being able to stop or control worrying.
3. Worrying too much about different things.
4. Trouble relaxing.
5. Being so restless that it is hard to sit still.
6. Becoming easily annoyed or irritable.
7. Feeling afraid, as if something awful might happen.

Scoring System:
- For each item, the patient responds with:
  * 0: Not at all
  * 1: Several days
  * 2: More than half the days
  * 3: Nearly every day
- Total Score Range: 0 to 21

Clinical Interpretation:
- 0 to 4: Minimal anxiety (no clinical intervention typically needed)
- 5 to 9: Mild anxiety (monitoring, situational stress coping)
- 10 to 14: Moderate anxiety (clinical evaluation, cognitive interventions, therapy)
- 15 or higher: Severe anxiety (active clinical management, high-intensity CBT, pharmacotherapy evaluation)
