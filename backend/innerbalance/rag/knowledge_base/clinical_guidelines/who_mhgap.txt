WHO MENTAL HEALTH GAP ACTION PROGRAMME (mhGAP) CLINICAL GUIDELINES
====================================================================

Core Principles of Care:
1. Promote respect, dignity, and human rights of people with mental health conditions.
2. Communicate in an empathetic, supportive, and non-judgmental tone.
3. Conduct clinical assessments including medical history, psychiatric history, and physical examination.

Depressive Disorders Assessment:
- Primary symptoms: Persistent low mood, marked loss of interest or pleasure in activities (anhedonia), and decreased energy or increased fatigue.
- Additional symptoms: Disturbed sleep or insomnia, changes in appetite or weight, anxiety or restlessness, reduced concentration, low self-esteem, feelings of guilt or worthlessness, and thoughts of self-harm or suicide.
- Severity scoring:
  * Moderate-to-Severe Depression: Presence of at least one primary symptom and a total of at least 4 symptoms in total, lasting for at least 2 weeks, with significant functional impairment.

Management of Depression:
- Psychoeducation: Educate patients and families about the nature of depression, expected recovery timeline, and importance of adherence to therapy.
- Psychosocial support: Offer problem-solving therapy, interpersonal therapy (IPT), cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), or behavioral activation.
- Pharmacological management: SSRIs (e.g. Fluoxetine, Amitriptyline) should be initiated for moderate-to-severe cases under supervision, monitoring for side effects and suicidal ideation, especially during the first few weeks of treatment.
- Follow-up: Regular review schedules (every 1-2 weeks initially) to track symptoms, treatment adherence, and safety.
